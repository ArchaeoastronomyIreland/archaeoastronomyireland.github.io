About Archaeoastronomy Ireland

Our initiative is dedicated to the study of archaeoastronomy across the Irish landscape. We utilize modern surveying techniques, digital modeling, and historical records to decode the astronomical significance of passage tombs, stone circles, and standing stones dating back to the Neolithic period. This field combines archaeology with celestial mechanics, revealing sophisticated knowledge of the cosmos held by Ireland's earliest inhabitants.

The precision of alignments—particularly those marking the winter solstice at sites like **Newgrange** and the equinoxes at **Knowth**—suggests a deeply ingrained cultural and ritualistic connection to the cycle of the seasons and the movement of the sun, moon, and stars. We aim to document, preserve, and communicate these findings to both the scientific community and the general public.

The project is inherently multidisciplinary, pulling expertise from archaeology, anthropology, engineering, and astronomy. By generating high-resolution digital models, we can test and confirm proposed alignments with unprecedented accuracy, minimizing human error and providing objective data. Our focus extends beyond the most famous sites to include lesser-known megalithic structures, ensuring a comprehensive regional understanding.
